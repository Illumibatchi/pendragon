## Summary
The squires remain at [[Winchbank Castle]], continuing their mission to observe [[Lady Ellen of Winchbank]] as a potential bride for Count Roderick. During a feast, [[Sir Blaine]] of Levcomagus (a Silchester knight) presents a finely crafted box containing a “rare creature” meant to impress the baron. When opened, the gift becomes an attack.

### The fairy dragon
A small dragon with butterfly-like wings—no larger than a small dog—erupts from the box and lunges for the [[Baron of Winchbank]]’s face. The squires react immediately:
- [[Noah]] grapples the creature to stop the assault
- [[Drustin]] helps pin its dangerous tail
- [[Millicent]] attempts to muzzle it with a cup
- [[Beonred (Beon)]] stands ready with a bench to defend the baron
- [[Assterius]] and [[Liam]] try to identify the creature

Liam recalls the creature is a **fairy dragon**, tied to the fairy realms—possessing a poisonous tail stinger and a strange mind-affecting breath. The squires hold it long enough for [[Sir Jerem]] to deliver the killing blow.

### Strange consequences
The creature’s blood is magical: it burns [[Drustin]] when it splashes him, but once washed away it leaves him strangely transformed—his appearance dramatically improved.

Sir Blaine falls to his knees begging forgiveness. He claims he bought the dragon egg from a mysterious woman in white, promised it would bring fortune to the baron’s house. Her name is revealed: **[[Lady Niev]]**.

Suspecting powerful faerie magic, the baron dismisses the Silchester knights and orders them to return home.

### Appeasing the fair folk
Fearing terrible misfortune from killing a fairy creature, the squires seek counsel from [[Lady Rachel of Carbonog]]. She warns the slaying is very bad luck and advises burying the dragon with offerings. The squires perform a ritual burial in a nearby forest:
- inter the dragon’s body
- leave food offerings
- sacrifice a cow
- bury [[Liam]]’s **silver dagger** (a gift from Lady Ellen) as part of the offering

### War comes to Salisbury
Before they can depart, word arrives: Vortigern’s heir marches with an Irish army against Salisbury. Moved by the squires’ courage and loyalty, the baron asks [[Sir Jerem]] to knight him so he can personally lead forces to aid Salisbury. Sir Jerem agrees; the baron raises an army, and the squires ride ahead with Sir Jerem toward Salisbury.

### The enemy at Figsbury — and the ogre’s return
Near [[Figsbury]], they spot a force approaching:
- ~40 Irish warriors
- javelin-throwing kerns
- a towering figure nearly three meters tall

The squires suspect the giant is the same ogre tied to the hillmen who killed their knights the previous year.

Sir Jerem proposes a risky plan: feint with the baggage train to distract, while the squires circle and strike the kerns from behind with hit-and-run tactics to split the enemy.

### Disaster in the charge
As the squires charge, the Irish spot them and hurl javelins. Horses panic; riders struggle for control. In the chaos, Noah charges to spear an enemy—misses—and is struck back by a kern with a dagger. The blow **decapitates Noah instantly**. The surviving squires press on as the battle has only begun.
